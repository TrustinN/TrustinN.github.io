Interests

Mathematics

Probably what defines my entire existence, perhaps. Some of my favorite books that I have read are Spivak's Calculus, Art and Craft of Problem Solving, and Euclid's Elements. I enjoy solving whatever problems I can get my hands on and have no clue what I want to study going forward. Yet these are the courses and related course notes that I have studied and created:

  • Linear Algebra
  • Abstract Algebra I
  • Mathematical Applications to Programming
  • Real Analysis
  • Algebraic Geometry
  • Combinatorics
  • Abstract Algebra II
Some of these do not have links because they were made before I configured neovim for typing notes (see the later section below). Probably my favorite class was combinatorics and the usage of generating functions to solve recursions. Such as finding an explicit formula for the Fibonacci Sequence, counting different types of partitions of objects and how it simplifies complex patterns are very appealing. Perhaps I'll take an algebraic combinatorics class next year :).

Programming

My largest addiction has been configuring a terminal editor called Neovim with the aim of typing up my latex notes faster. Yet it is one of my greatest joys to have a tool that I can understand the inner mechanics behind. I simply love the freedom of how much I can configure. The following are the major customizations that I have played around with:

Drawing Figures with Inkscape
This was completely inspired by an article from Gilles Castel about how he has used Inkscape to include figures in tex documents. From this I have learned how to utilize the cli from other software, along with using the telescope plugin for neovim to create, edit, and delete images.

Dynamically Controlled Matrices

These make typing up matrices and drawing commutative diagrams much more comfortable, compared to times when I was using Overleaf to type up my notes.
Extras
Some of the less productive features that I enjoy having in my editor are animated welcome screens using the chafa cli paired with alpha-nvim. Perhaps a more useful feature is being able to automatically tile my screen with my editor to my right and whatever window I want to the left, using hammerspoon. Again, with the power of telescope, I can have a picker to choose what window I want nvim to split with.
There are many other features that I am probably unaware of. Such as working with different compilers and lsp for different programming languages. As a newbie to coding, it is for sure a struggle to read through the docs to apply the code, yet it is completely worth it!

Piano

I am grateful for this hobby as it has pulled me through plenty of rough times. Sitting down at the piano, I can wash away all of my stress, and in the moment, I just want to play music. Although I had started my learning journey learning classical music, I bridged off into music that I enjoy, which are ones that I pull from Animenz. I love the clarity of the melody and the way that he makes the arrangement speak out. The music takes you on a journey, and at others, it drags from the sorrowful moments to ones of excitement. I play casually, but when it comes to having fun and relaxing, I take it seriously.

Games

My favorite games from my childhood have probably been from Bloons TD, and various other popular online games. One of my most memorable have been from discontinued games such as Robocraft and Battle Nations, yet they are in the works of renewed development whether it is from the original developer or someone else. Although I don't see myself getting back in, they remain apart of my fond memories of making friends online.
