Surface Reconstruction

Implicit and Explicit Reconstruction

In surface reconstruction, we attempt to recover a mesh that describes the surface from a point cloud. Reconstruction can be divided between two types of methods, one is implicit and the other explicit.

In explicit reconstruction, we construct the mesh through the physical properties of the mesh, for example, one method involves rolling a ball over the point cloud to define the surface. The crust method involves defining a local Delaunay triangulation to build a crust and buid the mesh that way.

Implicit reconstruction on the other hand defines a signed distance function. Through this, we can determine if a point in 3d space lies inside or outside of the mesh. A mesh generating algorithm such as marching cubes is then used to generate the mesh.

Regardless of the method, the aim is to build a watertight mesh that is robust to noise. Noise in this case is the inaccuracies in the point cloud that we are given. This can arise when our sampling tool we use to recover the point cloud gives inaccurate readings.
